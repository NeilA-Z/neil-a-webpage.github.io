A few things about me

In college, I pursued Electrical Engineering Technology, which, if you’re not familiar, sits somewhere between being a trade electrician and an engineer. As the saying goes, it’s the more budget-friendly alternative to engineering. However, my curiosity for technology didn’t begin in college—it started much earlier. I vividly remember spending time at the TV service center where my father worked, surrounded by exposed picture tubes and circuits. That experience completely reshaped the way I view the world.

Future Projects

In my spare time, I often find myself contemplating the untapped potential of technology, much like how television once revolutionized entertainment by solving the problem of boredom. Currently, I’m diving into the motor controller UMC100, paired with a VFD, with the goal of remotely controlling them via a webpage.

Project Update

I recently played around with an ABB AC500 PLC, and getting it up and running was more difficult than expected. The online instructions and update packages seemed outdated, and the schematics and documentation weren’t beginner-friendly. This might be intentional for cybersecurity reasons, but it also increases user reliance on the manufacturer. I hope ABB finds a better balance between security and user independence. Hopefully, my next project with the UMC100 won’t face the same issues—let’s see how it goes!
